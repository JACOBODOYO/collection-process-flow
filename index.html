Types of loan account states

ACTIVE LOANS:

This is a loan that is currently running on Mambu that is running well without any arrears, this means that we are getting the correct loan deduction from the client as per agreed terms.

LOANS IN ARREARS

This are loans which are currently running on the system but are in arrears where we are not getting deductions as required, It is important to understand the reason why an account is in arrears by checking when the loan started going in arrears by checking the repayments being made on the account.

There are various reasons for this such as:

  1. Client retired
  2. Salary stoppage
  3. Underpayment
  4. Deceased client
  5. Change of employment
  6. Fraud by client on payroll
  7. Change in affordability

BAD DEBT ACCOUNT/WRITE OFF

When a loan does not perform for more than 180 days, it is written off and a bad debt account is created for follow up, [this does not mean account is settled].

NO INTEREST ACCOUNT/ WRITE BACK

When a client has been re instated back to work and we are able to get decutions in subsiquent months the written off account is written back as a performing loan as a no interest account.

Skip Trace

Skip tracing is a process used to find a debtor who has relocated or disappeared to avoid legal responsibilities, such as debt payments.

The skip-tracing process involves verifying a debtors information, searching public records and contacting people who know the debtor.

Skip tracing is critical in the debt collection process, as it can help collectors locate clients who have gone MIA

SKIP TRACING TACTICS

It is important to note that the majority of information is now available online. i.e. Search engines and social media platforms.

Early steps of skip tracing can be completed by oneself.

Process to follow when the client is unreachable

  1. Skip trace client new contacts on crb report, nhif, Mpesa statement
  2. Contact next of keen, guarantors, referees,
  3. Look for more information of the client on his loan documents
  4. ID NUMBER/ BUSINESS NUMBER
  5. POSTAL ADDRESS/ EMAIL ADDRESS
  6. OCCUPATION
  7. Employer Information
  8. debt cards/notes
  9. Documents provided during loan application(loan forms)

Settlement Processes

For a client to settle his outstanding balance he is required to pay for statement Kshs 300. Then the statement is generated and is valid for 2 weeks. If a client wants to know the outstanding balance you calculate the interim statement, which will show the balance, Note this is not the settlement amount.

When the client clears his arrears initiate account closure, stop order and have a clearance letter attached on his account If there is any refunds make sure you contact the client on how he will get the refunds.

If the client is not able to pay the outstanding amount, you agree with him to start making partial payments each month until he clears, it is important to track the payments on the date agreed on

It is important to ensure te clients pay to the correct pay bill or account number for integrity perposes and payment follow ups.

Refinance and Buy offs

Refinance

Refince is the process for clearing the current loan that is running and creating a new loan so that the client can get a new loan. After the previous loan is settled the new loan starts with different terma and conditions

Buy Offs

This is a processes of buying a running loan from one institution to another.

INTERNAL BUY OFF

This is when an institution buys off loans from other banks

Calculating affordability

  1. Affordability means the amount which client can be able to use to apply for an amount of loan applied for after deductions and maintaining the a third rule required by law on the basic salary of a client,
  2. Must have a minimum third rule on basic salary against the net salary
  3. Checking on allowances to be uses {house, hardship. Transport medical, rental, transport], Note that some allowances are not considered depending on the work place.
  4. The use of tax relief 5% of salary.
  5. Age limit to be checked to the time of retire.

Interprating the payslip

  1. Check on the number of loans that are on the pay slip
  2. Balances of each facility given to the client
  3. CRB check to see if there are none performing loans not on pay slip
  4. Type of facilities personal loan, higher purchase loans, business expansion,
